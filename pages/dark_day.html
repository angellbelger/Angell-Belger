Dark day

Dark Day

  • Angell Belger

On May 14, 2022, Nowhere found itself the scene of a tragedy that would be recorded in its history. As a member of SUSP, I was assigned to work alongside the Chief Inspector, Mr. Duck. The day began with a tense stillness, soon dispelled when we were summoned for special weapons and shooting training. As night fell, we embarked on a patrol along with other security forces. During the course of the operation, we began to receive constant reports of attempted murders every 15 minutes.

The night unfolded chaotically, culminating in the sixth completed crime, revealing a scene of horror and helpless bodies. As we passed the crime scenes, those images stuck in my mind, movies on an eternal loop. Victims of a sinister plan, they became pawns in a plot to cover up the true motives behind the serial murders.

Returning to base, unable to identify the criminals, the disturbing images persisted in my mind. Surprisingly, I did not feel repulsed, but my body remained tied to the crime scene, unable to detach itself from the vivid scenes I witnessed.

In the dead of night, under the incessant rain that washed the streets of Nowhere, I remained there, a lonely specter from a recent past. The indifference that washed over me was like a thick fog, blurring the lines between right and wrong. The crime scenes echoed in my mind, a distorted symphony that mixed with the sound of the rain.

As my once dormant emotions began to awaken just 1 year after the incident, one thought echoed like thunder: death was not the end, but a constant reminder of the fragility of life. The rain, once a comfort, now became a relentless reminder of this fragility.

With the rain as a silent witness, an epiphany struck me like a bolt of lightning. It was time to abandon indifference, to recover lost caution. Fear, that visceral feeling, was, after all, the guardian of our humanity.

One morning, I stood up determined to face not only the horrors of the city, but also the demons that threatened my very essence.
