Bluff in the taxi

Bluff in the taxi

  • Angell Belger

On August 8, 2022, I returned to Osternack to face my last challenge. The trip was more than a geographic journey; it was the meticulous execution of plans and the reunion with family that permeated this experience.

One particular night, as I was leaving a meeting at the mall, I decided to call an Uber to my next destination. While I was waiting, a stranger approached me with an unusual compliment about my coat, comparing me to former President Donald Trump. Aware that such compliments often precede requests, I remained cautious.

The stranger, realizing my intention to call a taxi, asked to share the trip. I replied that this decision would be up to the driver, and he remained nearby. Taking advantage of a moment of his distraction, I discreetly walked around him, observing his posture and facial expression for clues about his intentions.

I focused on details like the possible presence of a weapon, analyzing waist height. Discreet observation led me to discard this hypothesis, since his clothing did not suggest the presence of visible weapons on the upper part of his body. I then tried to determine whether he was right-handed or left-handed, considering the potential risk of a serve. The difficulty in identifying the dominant arm indicated an uncertainty that added a layer of complexity to the enigma.

When trying to see if he was wearing a watch, I came across the limitation of long sleeves, making it impossible to draw a precise conclusion. The meeting, although brief, triggered a series of analyses, illustrating the constant readiness to face the unknown in search of security and discernment.

After calling the taxi, the stranger unceremoniously entered the car. During the trip, surprisingly, he revealed that he owned a 9mm gun, contrary to my previous analysis that had ruled out this possibility. Confused, I remained silent, pondering the true intentions of that unexpected passenger.

When declaring his real intentions, I considered arresting him, supported by my police power. At that moment, I assumed a more aggressive, although discreet, stance. His suspicious looks indicated that he might notice something out of the ordinary. The intriguing question about my occupation made me hesitate before answering.

Mother fu***... I'm a fu**ing full-stack. - Mysterious man

"I work as a software engineer," I said, a partial truth that omitted my broader role. His eyes, filled with disdain, cast judgment. With a pause, he continued, revealing himself to be a full-stack and expressing a peculiar mixture of respect and displeasure.

This brief dialogue, which unfolded over the course of a single trip, forced me to employ techniques of persuasion, behavioral analysis and non-verbal communication. Although tempted to act in the face of the threat, my decision was to leave the resolution to local authorities, considering the distance from my jurisdiction.

Uncertainty about the weapon's presence hung like a shadow in my mind, challenging confidence in my sharp analysis. Even though I had a mental palace that, countless times, provided me with clarity in my investigations, the heat of the moment made me doubt myself.

Revisiting the scene over and over in my mind, I delved into the nuances of that tense encounter. Every detail of the original analysis was dissected and examined in light of different perspectives. Emotions rose, reflecting the tension of a decision that could have significant impacts.

However, as I retraced the steps of that strange dialogue, my confidence in my initial conclusion resurfaced. He wasn't armed; he was just having fun, a subtle psychological game that I myself had used in several past investigations. The heat of the moment did not cloud my analytical ability; on the contrary, it revealed the resilience of the trust that sustains my mental palace.
